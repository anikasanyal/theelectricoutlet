BYD's Revolutionary “Blade Battery”

The Electric Outlet

28 May 2024 — 3 min read
Share


BYD, Chinese multinational giant pioneering renewable energy solutions, is electrifying the EV industry with its revolutionary "Blade Battery". This innovative technology promises to redefine safety standards, putting BYD at the forefront of a battery revolution.

BYD History: A Legacy in Battery Technology

BYD's roots are in rechargeable batteries. Founded in 1995, they started by producing nickel-cadmium batteries for mobile phones. As lithium-ion technology took hold, BYD shifted gears, becoming one of the world's largest producers of lithium-ion batteries for both consumer electronics and EVs. This deep understanding of battery technology positions them as a major player in the EV revolution.

The Blade Battery

The Blade Battery is a lithium iron phosphate (LFP) battery boasting a unique cell-to-pack design. Traditional EV battery packs consist of individual battery modules, which contain multiple battery cells.

The Blade Battery eliminates these modules, stacking elongated blade-shaped cells directly into the battery pack. This simplifies the structure, increases energy density (more range per charge), and, most importantly, enhances safety.

BYD's confidence in the Blade Battery's safety stems from its impressive performance in nail penetration tests, considered the most rigorous test for thermal runaway (battery overheating and fire). In these tests, the Blade Battery reportedly showed minimal temperature increase and no smoke or fire, a stark contrast to traditional lithium-ion batteries.

Traditional Battery Design: Cell > Module > Pack
BYD Blade Battery Design: Cell > Pack

Blade Cells: BYD utilizes elongated, flat cells referred to as "blade cells." These cells are designed with improved structural integrity

Pack Assembly: The blade cells are stacked directly together side-by-side, eliminating the need for separate modules. They are arranged in a way that provides structural support for the entire pack.
Increased Space Utilization: By eliminating modules, BYD claims to achieve a significant increase in space utilization within the pack, potentially up to 60% compared to the traditional design. This allows for more blade cells to be packed in, leading to higher energy density (more range per charge) for the overall battery pack.
Reduced Weight: The elimination of module casings translates to a lighter overall battery pack, improving vehicle efficiency.
Safety Benefits: The cell-to-pack design, along with the structural design of the blade cells themselves, contribute to improved safety in the event of a collision / puncture.

Battery Industry Titans: BYD vs. CATL

BYD faces stiff competition from industry titans like CATL (Contemporary Amperex Technology Co., Limited), another Chinese battery giant. CATL, a major supplier to Tesla, is also a leader in LFP battery technology.

Focus: BYD is both a battery manufacturer and a leading EV producer, allowing them to tailor the Blade Battery specifically for their BYD vehicles. CATL primarily focuses on supplying batteries to external car companies.
Technology Specifics: Both BYD and CATL utilize LFP chemistry; BYD highlights their cell-to-pack design, as a key safety innovation and CATL offers various LFP battery options with a focus on high performance and lifespan.

Battery Types: LFP vs. Lithium-ion / Nickel cobalt manganese (NCM) batteries

Safety: Due to the iron phosphate chemistry, LFP batteries are more stable, which translates to a lower risk of thermal runaway - a major concern with traditional lithium-ion batteries. [Winner: LFP]
Cost: LFP batteries use more readily available materials compared to NCM batteries, making them cheaper to produce. [Winner: LFP]
Lifespan: LFP batteries generally boast a longer lifespan than NCM batteries, offering more charging cycles before degradation. [Winner: LFP]
Energy Density: NCM batteries currently offer higher energy density, translating to potentially greater range on a single charge. [Winner: NCM]
Charging Speed: NCM batteries may also support faster charging speeds compared to LFP batteries. [Winner: NCM]

The Blade Battery's cell-to-pack design represents a significant leap forward in EV battery technology. With companies like BYD pushing boundaries in LFP technology, the future of EVs looks brighter, safer, and more exciting for consumers and the environment alike.
